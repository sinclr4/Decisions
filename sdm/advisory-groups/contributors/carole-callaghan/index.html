CAROLE CALLAGHAN

Advanced Clinical Pharmacist (Rheumatology)

Ms Carole Callaghan is a Specialist Clinical Pharmacist for Rheumatology in NHS Lothian and has worked in rheumatology since 2000. Carole graduated with a BSc (Hons) Pharm from the University of Strathclyde, Glasgow in 1993 and completed her MSc in Clinical Pharmacy here in 1994. She subsequently qualified as an Independent Pharmacist Prescriber in 2007.

In 2006, Carole founded the Scottish Rheumatology Pharmacists’ Network, which now has members from throughout the NHS Boards in Scotland. This provides peer review and support for pharmacists from secondary care specialising in rheumatology and is the platform for the dissemination and implementation of national policies and strategies relating to the care of patients with rheumatological conditions.

Since 2007, Carole has been an Honorary Lecturer at the School of Pharmacy, University of Strathclyde, Glasgow, providing teaching to undergraduate and postgraduate pharmacy students. Carole is also involved in providing teaching to other healthcare professionals, locally and nationally. She is actively involved in the development and delivery of various clinical audits and practice research projects and is currently undertaking a doctorate. Carole was a member of the Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network committee for SIGN 123 (The Management of Early Rheumatoid Arthritis) and is currently on the committee for the review of SIGN 71 (The Management of Osteoporosis).
