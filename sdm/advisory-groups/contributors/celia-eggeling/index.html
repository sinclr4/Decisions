CELIA EGGELING

Lead Psychosocial Care

I qualified as a social worker in 1980 and worked as a local authority generic social worker until 1986 when I transferred over to the S.W.Thames Renal Unit, which is where I still practice. Recognising the adjustments patients and families have to make to integrate renal replacement therapies into their lives, and the lack of psychological support available, I trained as a psychotherapist gaining a Doctorate in Psychotherapy in 2002.

My publications are :

2010 - Supportive Care for Renal Patients - OUP
2007 - End of Life Care in Nephrology - OUP
2007 - Palliative Care in Nephrology - Nephrology, Dialysis and
Transplantation
2003 - Psychological Care in Advanced Renal Care.
1999 - Psychological Implications of Living Kidney Donation - BJRM/EDTNA
