DAVID ARDRON

Lay representative

David has worked widely in consumer affairs in lung cancer research now for eight years. His parents were cancer patients, his father with lung cancer and his mother with breast cancer. He is chair of the North Trent Cancer Research Network Consumer Research Panel, and a member of the North Trent Cancer Network Patient Partnership Group. He was a lay member of the National Cancer Research Institute (NCRI) Lung Cancer Clinical Studies Group for five years, and Chair of the NCRI Consumer Liaison Group from June 2008 to May 2012. He was a member of the NCRI PET Scanning Research Strategic Planning Group. He has been a member of the trial management group for the MRC QUARTZ trial in Lung Cancer since 2004, and also the UK Lung Cancer Screening Trial since 2009. He is interested in the practical role that local consumer research panels have to play in health research, and has been invited to describe the work of the North Trent Panel and the Consumer Liaison Group at many meetings and conferences. His background is in science teaching and computer literacy. He sits as a member of other National Cancer Initiatives including, NICE, NCIN, and the National Lung Data Audit, and works as a lay member of the Yorkshire and Humber Deanery. He has also recently been appointed to help as a facilitator for patient and public involvement in the South Yorkshire Comprehensive Local Research Network.
