DERMOT O'RIORDA

Medical Advisory Group Lead: Consultant surgeon and medical director

Dermot O’Riordan was appointed as consultant surgeon in 2001 and as medical director in 2009 at West Suffolk NHS Foundation Trust. Prior to this he was clinical director for surgical services and then deputy medical director. Dermot is the Trust’s responsible officer for revalidation. He qualified in medicine from Bart’s in 1988.

In 2003 he was selected as one of the Health Foundation’s inaugural Leadership Fellows.

Also in 2003 he became the youngest elected member of Council and Trustee at the Royal College of Surgeons of England. He chaired the College’s reconfiguration working party.

He was invited to be a member of the NHS Future Forum, contributing to the group initially looking at choice and competition and in its second phase the group working on integration. Dermot is a member of the Clinical Information Advisory Group to NHS Choices (chairing its Data Working Party) and the Clinical Advisory Panel on Payment by Results to the Department of Health. He chairs the group of medical directors (and ROs) in the East of England. He has been president of the Association of Surgeons in Training.

Outside of work he is into cycling and sailing. In 2008 and 2010 he sailed in the Arctic in Greenland and through the NW Passage in Canada and Alaska. He regularly skippers boats in the NHS Regatta, and reliably finishes in the middle of the fleet.
