HILARY BEKKER

External advisor: Associate Professor, Leeds Institute of Health Sciences - University of Leeds, UK

Dr Hilary Bekker is a health psychologist with expertise in medical decision making. Hilary investigates the science behind patient decision aid interventions and their use in clinical practice. Hilary has worked with national and international organisations (NICE; NHS; International Patient Decision Aid Standards (IPDAS) consortium) to develop and implement guidelines for staff and researchers to support patients making informed decisions about healthcare options. 

Hilary is course lead for the Individuals and Population year 1 course of the medical degree, University of Leeds. Hilary’s primary research focus is on translating the knowledge psychologists have about how people process information, what biases people’s choices and why interventions help people make better decisions to the health context. Hilary has a PhD in Decision Making about Prenatal Diagnosis (University of Leeds, 1999), an MSc in Health Psychology (University College London, 1994) and a BSc Psychology (first class) (Middlesex Polytechnic, 1990).
