JAMES CALVERT

Consultant Respiratory Physician

James Calvert qualified in Medicine from Oxford University in 1992 before undertaking posts in Oxford and Newcastle. He then spent a year at Harvard University as a Fulbright Scholar studying for a Masters Degree in Public Health. This was followed by experience in a wide variety of posts in Edinburgh. He then became a Clinical Fellow for 14 months on the Scottish Adult Cystic Fibrosis Service before gaining a Wellcome Training Fellowship to undertake a study of Asthma. This was conducted over 3 years between the University of Cape Town and Kings College London. This work was awarded a PhD in 2004.

He was appointed as a Consultant in Bristol in 2006. His clinical interests encompass all areas of Respiratory Medicine, particularly asthma/allergic disease, COPD & lung infection.

Dr Calvert is currently Lead Clinician in Respiratory Medicine and Deputy Medical Director at North Bristol NHS Trust. He chairs the Professional and Organisational Standards Committee at the British Thoracic Society and is one of the Respiratory Leads at the South of England Strategic Health Authority.
