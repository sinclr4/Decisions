JOANNE M JERROME

Deputy CEO, AF Association (AF-A)

Jo qualified as a teacher in 1987 and practised for more than twenty years across the UK, working with special needs pupils and teenagers excluded from educational settings, and with severely disabled adults.

In 2007, she was approached to assist the launch the Syncope Trust And Reflex Anoxic Seizures’ (www.stars.org.uk) Shine A light On Education website. As part of this, Jo has been involved in publishing care plans and guidance booklets, organising training sessions for volunteer representatives, and developing support for families and educational establishments.

Later that year, Jo supported the development of a charity specifically for patients with atrial fibrillation (AF), called the AF Association (AF-A). She has been instrumental in lobbying for government support by developing the All-Party Parliamentary Group for AF (APGAF), worked in alliance with global organisations to raise awareness of AF and AF-related stroke, and published comprehensive Department of Health endorsed information material for patients and physicians. Jo has been the driving force to develop annual AF Patient Days, which now run alongside clinical meetings in the UK and abroad, as well as establishing an exclusive website for healthcare professionals with an interest in AF (www.heartofaf.org).

Jo sits on numerous medical boards, including: Co-chair All Party Parliamentary Group on AF, National Institute Clinical Excellence Guideline Committee for Atrial Fibrillation, NHS/BMJ Patient Decision Making Aid for stroke prevention in atrial fibrillation, Steering Group – British Heart Foundation Teen Site, NICE Appraisal Committee for Single Technology, dronedarone, AF Aware member, Member of the Alliance for Stroke Prevention in AF, Member of PDA AF-Medical Advisory Group, Anticoagulation Alliance, NICE TA expert panel member Dabigatran, NICE TA expert panel member Rivaroxaban, NICE TA expert panel member Apixaban, and NICE Commissioning Anticoagulation Services.
