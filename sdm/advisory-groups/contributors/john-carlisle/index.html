Dr John Carlisle

45 years old, monthly mortality risk about 1/7000, survival risk 6999/7000. Adjusted median life expectancy 44 (more) years. I’m married with a 3 year-old daughter.

I work in Torbay hospital as a consultant in perioperative preparation, anaesthesia & critical care.

Main areas of interest: perioperative quantification of risk; shared decision-making; cardiopulmonary exercise testing; evidence-based medicine including systematic reviews; detection of data fabrication (see May’s issue Anaesthesia; http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1365-2044.2012.07128.x/full Editor for Anaesthesia (appointed May 2012). Editor & author for the Cochrane collaboration (Anaesthesia Review Group).. Adviser to the Royal College of Anaesthetists on perioperative medicine. ESA & ESICM evidence-based guideline groups. Author for Oxford Handbooks of: anaesthesia; day surgery; vascular surgery. Instructor on biannual 2-day cardiopulmonary exercise workshop course.
