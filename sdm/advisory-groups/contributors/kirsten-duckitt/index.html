KIRSTEN DUCKITT

Gynaecologist

Kirsten Duckitt qualified in Medicine from Cambridge University and University College Hospital/Middlesex Hospital School of Medicine. During her training in Obstetrics and Gynaecology, she worked as a clinical research fellow at the Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists where she helped develop the RCOG National Evidence-based Guidelines on menorrhagia, infertility and sterilisation. These subsequently helped form the basis for several NICE guidelines. Having been appointed consultant at the John Radcliffe Hopsital, Oxford in 2002, she moved to Canada in 2004, back to a consultant post in Milton Keynes in 2008 only to return again to Canada in 2010. She is now practising as a general Obstetrician and Gynaecologist in a rural area on Vancouver Island, British Columbia. She is a Clinical Assistant Professor in the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology at the University of British Columbia (UBC). She continues to contribute to the BMJ Clinical Evidence series and remains active in the Cochrane Menstrual Disorders and Subfertility and the Cochrane Pregnancy and Childbirth Groups.
