LOU MARSDEN

Down's Syndrome Association: Training Officer

Lou Marsden is a Training Officer for the Down’s Syndrome Association, a charity working throughout England, Wales and Northern Ireland To create the conditions that all people with Down’s syndrome need to live full and rewarding lives.

Lou developed and delivers the ‘Tell it Right, Start it Right’ RCM accredited study day for midwives, maternity and other related health professionals designed to ensure that health professionals offer balanced, accurate and up to date information about Down’s syndrome to expectant and new parents.

Lou’s professional background in Learning Disability Nursing (RNLD 1985 – 1999) has influenced her training in all aspects of Down’s syndrome from before birth to end of life. Her particular interests are in health, employment, supporting adults, ageing and dementia. Lou was a member of the PAG on the ‘Supporting Derek’ training package developed by Edinburgh University and published by the Joseph Rowntree Foundation and I was a am member of the T21 National Standards Working Group.
