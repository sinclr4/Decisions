MARK HADDAD

Senior Lecturer in Mental Health; Specialist Practitioner (mental health nursing)

Mark Haddad trained initially as an adult (general) nurse at the Royal London Hospital, and later as a mental nurse at Guy's & Lewisham Hospitals. He has a specialist interest in the relationship between mental and physical health and maintains a clinical role in primary care mental health.
In January 2012, he was appointed Senior Lecturer in Mental Health at City University London. Previously he was a Clinical Research Fellow at the Institute of Psychiatry, King's College London. In 2002 he was awarded an MRC Clinical Fellowship to develop and evaluate training for the mental health work of district nurses, and this randomised controlled trial formed his PhD study. His post-doctoral studies have concerned evaluating training for school nurses work with adolescent depression; exploring the relationship between coronary heart disease and depression and developing and evaluating interventions for this combination of problems; and exploring clinicians' attitudes to depression and its treatment. Mark was a member of the NICE guideline development group for depression and chronic physical illness (2008-9). He is an elected national committee member of the RCN Mental Health Forum, and the author of 30 articles and 4 book chapters on mental health topics, as well as a book on depression.
