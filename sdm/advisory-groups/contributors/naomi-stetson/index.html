NAOMI STETSON

Independent Nurse Consultant in Hypertension and Cardiovascular Disease

Naomi Stetson is from London, and qualified as an RN in 1981 from University College Hospital.

She has been a Primary Care nurse for 20 years and a Clinical Nurse Specialist in Hypertension at the Royal Free Hospital. Her main interest is Hypertension and Cardiovascular disease. She is currently chair of the British Hypertension Society Nurses Working Party and an Executive member of the BHS. She has completed further specialist training at the University of Glasgow, and is also a non-medical prescriber. She currently assists with the running of Hypertension Study days for nurses and non-medical prescribing training for nurses and pharmacists.

She was a member of the NICE Hypertension Guideline Development Group and Topic Expert Group; she was also on a PCT lead nurse group looking at strategic planning for Practice Nurses.

Currently she is producing training modules for the multidisciplinary team in Primary Care under the umbrella of Msbnursingsolutions, which is a training company she set up last year.
