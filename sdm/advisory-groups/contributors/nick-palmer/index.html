NICK PALMER

Head of Advocacy, National Kidney Federation

Nick has worked for the National Kidney Federation since November 2010. In 2012, as Head of Advocacy he has overseen the expansion of the service from three to eight officers. Nick is involved in wide ranging work; helping and representing patients with individual problems; assisting Kidney Patient Associations; working closely with Renal Networks, the Department of Health, NHS Kidney Care, NHS Blood and Transplant and related organisations and charities. Nick also acts as a Lay Member for NICE on guideline development groups. Nick is passionate about representing the interests of all patients and as a kidney transplant patient himself uses his insight to great effect. He is particularly interested in live donation and immunosuppression, home haemodialysis, the benefits of exercise for renal patients and psychological support for renal patients.

 

Nick has a degree in Geography and a Post Graduate Diploma in Urban & Regional Planning, before working for the NKF Nick had a career in Town Planning & Regeneration undertaking assignments in policy, development, research and consultation on behalf of private and public sector organisations.
