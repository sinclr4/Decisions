PHILIPPA MARSDEN

Consultant Obstetrician with an interest in Maternal Medicine.

Dr Philippa Marsden is a Consultant Obstetrician with an interest in Maternal Medicine at the Royal Victoria Infirmary, Newcastle upon Tyne.

She graduated from Newcastle in 1985. During her training in Obstetrics and Gynaecology in the Northeast, she undertook research leading to her MD thesis on Insulin action and ovarian function. She was appointed as a Consultant Obstetrician and Gynaecologist to the University Hospital of North Durham in 1999 where she was RCOG Tutor for many years but moved to Newcastle in 2011 to develop her interest in medical education and maternal medicine.

She has had an interest in producing good quality information for patients for many years and is currently Chair of the Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists Patient Information Committee. As a member of the committee, before taking up her role as Chair, she was part of the multidisciplinary group that developed the “Recovering Well Series’ for gynaecological procedures with the Department of Work and Pensions. She is on the advisory group for a PhD student: ‘Breech presentation: a decision aid for deciding mode of delivery’.

She is currently ‘Course Director for Women’s Health’ for the Newcastle upon Tyne MBBS programme as well as undergraduate lead for Women’s Health for the Tyne Base. Her clinical interests are obstetric haematology and obstetric neurology.
