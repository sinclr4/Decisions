RICHARD SEAL

Chief Pharmacist and clinical lead for medicines optimisation

Richard Seal is a registered pharmacist who studied at Aston School of Pharmacy and the University of Liverpool. He has a postgraduate diploma in clinical pharmacy and pharmacokinetics and a Masters degree in Prescribing Sciences.

Richard was recently appointed to the post of Chief Pharmacist and clinical lead for medicines optimisation with the NHS Trust Development Authority.

Richard joined West Midlands Strategic Health Authority as Programme Consultant in Medicines Management in September 2008 and was also Medicines Management Adviser to the NHS Institute for Innovation and Improvement between 2007 and 2010. Previously, he was Director of Medicines Management at the National Prescribing Centre in Liverpool.

Richard takes a keen interest in professional pharmacy matters and is lead for Birmingham and Solihull Local Pharmacy Forum of the Royal Pharmaceutical Society.

His special interests include the quality use of medicines including governance and medication safety, organisational change and development, innovation and leadership for medicines optimisation and professional development.
