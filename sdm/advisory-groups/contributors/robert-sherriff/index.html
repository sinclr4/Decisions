ROBERT SHERRIFF

Consultant in Public Health Medicine

Robert qualified in medicine from Charing Cross and Westminster Medical School, University of London. He trained in public health medicine in East Anglia and studied at the University of Cambridge. Whilst completing his training in public health medicine, Robert helped set up the UK National Screening Committee in 1996 and developed the UK NSC criteria for judging the effectiveness of population screening programmes.

In 1999, Robert was appointed Director of Public Health for Buckinghamshire and Milton Keynes and in 2002 he became Director of Public Health / Medical Director for the Thames Valley Strategic Health Authority. In 2006, Robert joined the UK NSC as the National Co-ordinator for Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm Screening and went onto chair the national AAA Screening Implementation Group. Since 2008, Robert has been providing advice on behalf of the UK NSC to the Vascular Risk Management Programme of the Department of Health.
