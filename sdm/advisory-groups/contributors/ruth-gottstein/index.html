RUTH GOTTSTEIN

Consultant Neonatologist

Dr Ruth Gottstein studied medicine at Trinity College Dublin, Ireland and qualified in 1993. After working in Neonatology and Obstetrics & Gynaecology in Coombe Women’s Hospital, Dublin, Ruth gained further postgraduate paediatric and neonatal training in Northwest England. Her subspecialty training in Neonatology, Paediatric Intensive Care, Developmental Paediatrics and Cardiology was at Liverpool Women’s Hospital, Alder Hey Children’s Hospital and St. Mary’s Hospital, Manchester. Ruth took up a consultant post at the tertiary Newborn Intensive Care Unit at St. Mary’s Hospital in 2003. In 2008 Ruth gained an MSc in Child Health from University of Liverpool. She has published a systematic review and meta-analysis on the effect of intravenous immunoglobulin in Haemolytic Disease of the Newborn. Ruth’s subspecialty interests include neonatal haematology, neonatal echocardiography and teaching particularly simulation training. She is currently involved in the development of an Advanced Resuscitation of the Newborn Infant (ARNI) Course under the auspices of the Resuscitation Council UK. Ruth is RCPCH tutor for neonatal training at St. Mary’s Hospital, Manchester (2010-2013) and honorary secretary of Manchester Paediatric Club (2012 – 2015).
