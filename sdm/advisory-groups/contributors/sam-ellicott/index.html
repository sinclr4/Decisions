SAM ELLICOTT

Patient representative

In November 2010 I was driving my car whilst listening to a local radio programme on which was featured an interview with a local Consultant Vascular Surgeon and the subject being discussed was
the introduction of a local screening programme for men at age of 65 who would be receiving a letter on their birthday. I was only giving slight attention to the interview but I do remember that the interviewer pointed out that he was nearer 70 and would therefore not receive a letter. This applied to me also and so, when a telephone number for the local screening service was given out, I made a note and rang the office the next day. An ultrasound scan was arranged for the next week and I was found to have a large abdominal aortic aneurysm – 6.5cm. I was seen by a Consultant two days later and an open surgical repair was performed in early December 2010.

I firmly believe that this prompt action saved my life and therefore I am eager to assist in any way possible to spread the word about the screening programme and encourage men to take that essential decision to attend for screening and not hesitate. If I had hesitated I may not have had such a happy and successful outcome.
