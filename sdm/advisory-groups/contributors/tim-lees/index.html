TIM LEES

Consultant Vascular Surgeon and Research Lead of NHS AAA Screening Programme

Mr Tim Lees has been a consultant in Newcastle since 1995 having previously trained in Manchester, Norwich, Newcastle, Sheffield and the USA. He is also an Associate Clinical Researcher at the University of Newcastle upon Tyne. He qualified from Manchester University in 1984, was awarded his FRCS England in 1989 and an MD with Commendation from Newcastle University in 1993.

Within vascular surgery he has a particular interest in the investigation and management of varicose veins and complex venous disease, including vascular malformations. In association with his colleagues he has been at the forefront of the development of new techniques in the management of venous disease and has taught nationally and internationally on venous ultrasound. He has been secretary of the Venous Forum of the Royal Society of Medicine

Mr Lees is committed to audit and improving clinical outcomes He has previously chaired the Audit and Research Committee of the Vascular Society of Great Britain and Ireland (VSGBI) and led a successful grant application to the Health Foundation for national quality improvement in Aneurysm surgery. He is now the regional lead for this programme. He has chaired the UKcarotid endarterectomy audit with Royal College of Physicians and VSGBI 2005-9. He is current chair of the international vascular audit group, Vascunet. He is the clinical lead of a vascular expert working group on coding& HRGs. He is currently the research lead for the NHS abdominal aortic aneurysm screening programme, and sits on the Research Committee of the VSGBI.

He has published widely in Vascular Surgery and has been principle supervisor of several Newcastle MD students.
