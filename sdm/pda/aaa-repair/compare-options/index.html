MORE INFORMATION ABOUT ABDOMINAL AORTIC ANEURYSM (AAA) REPAIR

What is the aorta?

The aorta is the main artery that supplies blood to your body. It is about the width of a garden hose and runs from your heart down through your chest and abdomen before branching off into smaller blood vessels.

What is an abdominal aortic aneurysm?

As you get older, the wall of your aorta can become weak, causing it to balloon outwards and form a bulge. The bulge is called an abdominal aortic aneurysm. Most aneurysms develop in the lower part of the aorta below the kidneys. The condition is most common in men aged 65 and older. You might also hear it called AAA or triple A.

Abdominal aortic aneurysms tend to get larger over time. Most grow slowly but some can increase in size quite suddenly.

If an aneurysm is small when it is detected, it may never grow big enough to cause problems. But large aneurysms can be dangerous. If the wall of the aorta gets very weak it might burst, causing massive internal bleeding. This can be fatal.

An aneurysm is considered large if it is at least 5.5 centimetres wide at its largest point. When an aneurysm reaches this size, surgery is usually recommended. This is because, at this size, the risk of rupture is usually higher than the risk from surgery.[1]Moll FL, Powell JT, Fraedrich G, et al. Management of abdominal aortic aneurysms clinical practice guidelines of the European Society for Vascular Surgery. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2011; 41 (supplement 1): S1-S58.

What are the symptoms of abdominal aortic aneurysm?

You will probably not notice any symptoms if you have an abdominal aortic aneurysm. Most people don’t feel any pain or notice anything different.

However, occasionally people get pain in their abdomen, loin, or lower back, which can be a sign that an aneurysm is about to rupture. Signs that an aneurysm has ruptured include abdominal pain, low blood pressure, and collapse.[2]Metcalfe D, Holt PJ, Thompson MM, et al. The management of abdominal aortic aneurysms. BMJ. 2011; 342: d1384.

Why has this happened to me?

We don’t know exactly what causes the wall of the aorta to weaken, leading to an abdominal aortic aneurysm. However, several things can increase your risk of this happening.

Men aged 65 and older are more likely to get an abdominal aortic aneurysm than younger men. And men aged 65 and older are much more likely to have an aneurysm than women.[3]Scott RA, Bridgewater SG, Ashton HA. Randomized clinical trial of screening for abdominal aortic aneurysm in women. British Journal of Surgery. 2002; 89; 283–285.

You are also more at risk if:

  • You smoke. You are 5 to 15 times more likely to develop an aneurysm if you have ever smoked. The risk increases the more you have smoked.[4]Nordon IM, Hinchliffe RJ, Loftus IM, et al. Pathophysiology and epidemiology of abdominal aortic aneurysms. Nature Reviews Cardiology. 2011; 8: 92-102.[5]Lederle FA, Johnson GR, Wilson SE, et al. The aneurysm detection and management study screening program: validation cohort and final results. Aneurysm Detection and Management Veterans Affairs Cooperative Study Investigators. Archives of Internal Medicine. 2000; 160: 1425-1230.
  • Your brother, sister, or parent has or has had an abdominal aortic aneurysm. You may be 2 to 8 times more likely to develop an aneurysm if you have a close family member with the condition.[6]Larsson E, Granath F, Swedenborg J, Hultgren R. A population-based case-control study of the familial risk of abdominal aortic aneurysm. J Vasc Surg. 2009. 49; 47-50.[7]Salo JA, Soisalon-Soininen S, Bondestam S, et al. Journal of Vascular Surgery. 2005; 42: 891-897.
  • You have high blood pressure. You may be nearly twice as likely to develop an aneurysm if you’ve had high blood pressure for a while.[9]Vardulaki KA, Walker NM, Day NE. Quantifying the risks of hypertension, age, sex and smoking in patients with abdominal aortic aneurysm. British Journal of Surgery. 2000; 87: 195-200.
  • You are very overweight (obese).   • You are white. White men have a higher risk of an abdominal aortic aneurysm than men who are Asian or black.[12]Metcalfe D, Holt PJ, Thompson MM. The management of abdominal aortic aneurysms. BMJ. 2011; 342: d1384.
How do I know I have an abdominal aortic aneurysm?

Abdominal aortic aneurysms are often spotted during a routine physical examination when a GP feels a bulge in the abdomen. They may also be found through tests done for other reasons, such as chest X-rays for lung problems.

If your doctor thinks you may have an abdominal aortic aneurysm, you’ll have an ultrasound scan to confirm the diagnosis and check the size of the aneurysm.

Some aneurysms go undetected until they cause serious problems. To spot more aneurysms early on, the NHS has launched a screening programme for men aged 65 or over. The NHS invites all men for screening in the year they turn 65. 

How many people have an abdominal aortic aneurysm?

Abdominal aortic aneurysms are rare in people under the age of 60. But around 5 in 100 men aged 65 to 74 have these aneurysms.[Thompson SG, Ashton HA, Gao L, et al. BMJ. 2009 Jun 24;338:b2307. doi: 10.1136/bmj.b2307.] The risk increases with age.

Men are six times more likely to get abdominal aortic aneurysms than women,[13]Scott RA, Bridgewater SG, Ashton HA. Randomized clinical trial of screening for abdominal aortic aneurysm in women. British Journal of Surgery. 2002; 89, 283-285. although some research suggests that these aneurysms are becoming more common in women as well.[4]Nordon IM, Hinchliffe RJ, Loftus IM, et al. Pathophysiology and epidemiology of abdominal aortic aneurysms. Nature Reviews Cardiology. 2011; 8: 92-102.

It’s hard to say with certainty how many people have large aneurysms. Programmes that screen older men for aneurysms suggest that about 5 out every 1,000 have an aneurysm that is large (5.5 centimetres or wider). Aneurysms this size have a higher risk of rupturing.[15]Ashton HA, Buxton MJ, Day NE, et al. The Multicentre Aneurysm Screening Study (MASS) into the effect of abdominal aortic aneurysm screening on mortality in men: a randomised controlled trial. Lancet. 2002; 360: 1531-1539. [16]Norman PE, Jamrozik K, Lawrence-Brown MM, et al. Population based randomised controlled trial on impact of screening on mortality from abdominal aortic aneurysm. BMJ. 2004; 329: 1259.

What will happen?

Small aneurysms are unlikely to cause any problems. With larger aneurysms, the risk is that they may rupture (burst). Fewer than 20 in 100 people survive a ruptured aneurysm.[2]Metcalfe D, Holt PJ, Thompson MM, et al. The management of abdominal aortic aneurysms. BMJ. 2011; 342: d1384. Doctors usually can’t say for certain whether an aneurysm will rupture. But they can tell you whether your aneurysm is at higher risk.

If your aneurysm is at least 5.5 centimetres wide, it has a greater chance of bursting than a smaller aneurysm, and the risk increases as your aneurysm grows. FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT OPEN ANEURYSM REPAIR

What is open aneurysm repair?

In an open aneurysm repair, a surgeon makes a large cut into your abdomen to reach your aorta directly. The surgeon then cuts open the aneurysm and stitches a piece of fabric, called a graft, inside. The aneurysm is then closed with the graft inside.

The graft strengthens the part of the aorta with the aneurysm, carrying blood through this weakened section.

You have a general anaesthetic during the surgery, so you won’t be awake.

In some hospitals, this type of repair can be done by laparoscopic surgery, using a tiny camera and small instruments to operate through smaller cuts.

After you have recovered from the operation, you shouldn’t need further treatment or follow-up scans.

Who can choose open repair?

Most large abdominal aortic aneurysms are suitable for open repair. But the surgery isn’t suitable for every person, as it’s a major operation with serious risks. You need to be healthy enough to survive the operation and benefit from it. If you have other health conditions, such as heart or lung disease, the operation may not help you live longer, so it may not be worth the risks.

What are the effects of open repair on your risk of rupture?

Having open repair lowers your risk of having a ruptured aneurysm, compared with not having surgery. Ruptures after open surgery are not common.[24]Powell JT, Brown LC, Forbes JF, et al. Final 12-year follow-up of surgery versus surveillance in the UK Small Aneurysm Trial. British Journal of Surgery. 2007; 94: 702-708.

In one group of almost 600 people who had open surgery for a large aneurysm, none of them had a rupture during nearly five years of follow-up.[25]Wyss TR, Brown LC, Powell JT, et al. Rate and predictability of graft rupture after endovascular and open abdominal aortic aneurysm repair: data from the EVAR Trials. Annals of Surgery. 2010; 252: 805-812.

The risk of rupture for untreated large aneurysms is higher. Within a year, up to 15 in 100 aneurysms measuring 5.5 to 6 centimetres will rupture. And the risk increases with aneurysm size. Up to 50 in 100  aneurysms 8 centimetres and larger will rupture within a year.[26]British Heart Foundation. Abdominal aortic aneurysms. Factfile No. 1. 2008. Available at http://www.bhf.org.uk/idoc.ashx?docid=f6657a6e-d1c7-485d-aaae-77e5868c6309&version=-1 (accessed 6 June 2012).

What are the effects of open repair on your length of life?

Open aneurysm repair can help you live longer by reducing the risk of your aneurysm rupturing.

Around 70 in 100 people are still alive six years after open surgery for a large aneurysm. Among those who’ve died, some have died of problems related to their aneurysm and some have died of other causes.[27]De Bruin JL, Baas AF, Buth J, et al. Long-term outcome of open or endovascular repair of abdominal aortic aneurysm. New England Journal of Medicine. 2010; 362: 1881-1889.

In comparison, around 20 in 100 people are still alive after five years if they don’t have surgery to repair a large aneurysm.[28]National Institute for Health and Clinical Excellence. TA167 Abdominal aortic aneurysm - endovascular stent-grafts: guidance. January 2012. Available at http://www.nice.org.uk/Guidance/TA167 (accessed on 10 May 2012).

All surgeries carry a risk of dying during or soon after the operation. Around 4 in 100 people die during open repair surgery or in the 30 days after surgery.[29]United Kingdom EVAR Trial Investigators, Greenhalgh RM, Brown LC, et al. Endovascular versus open repair of abdominal aortic aneurysm. New England Journal of Medicine. 2010; 362: 1863-1871.

Your chance of dying may be higher or lower based on your health and age. In one group of people aged 80 and older, nearly 9 in 100 died in hospital after an open repair.[30]Biancari F, Catania A, D'Andrea V, et al. Elective endovascular vs. open repair for abdominal aortic aneurysm in patients aged 80 years and older: systematic review and meta-analysis. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2011; 42: 571-576. People who are younger and in good health have a lower risk.

What are the effects of open repair on what you can do?

Most people with an abdominal aortic aneurysm have no symptoms. So having their aneurysm repaired doesn’t change how they feel physically or what they can do once they recover from the operation. 

Time in hospital

People spend around seven days in hospital after open repair.[31]Chambers D, Epstein D, Walker S, et al. Endovascular stents for abdominal aortic aneurysms: a systematic review and economic model. Health Technology Assessement. 2009; 13: 1-189. This may include one or two days in the intensive care unit (ICU).

Time to full recovery

A full recovery often takes between three and six months after open repair.[32]Circulation Foundation. Endovascular Aneurysm Repair (EVAR). Available at http://www.circulationfoundation.org.uk/help-advice/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm/endovascular-aneurysm-repair-evar/ (accessed on 10 May 2012). But people vary in how they respond. Some people will recover more quickly and others will not feel fully recovered a year or longer after their surgery.

Your recovery time will depend in large part on your age, health, and activity level before surgery. If you have complications from your operation, it may also take you longer to recover.

Many people can return to work within six to 12 weeks of open surgery.[33]Circulation Foundation. Open AAA Repair Operation. Available at http://www.circulationfoundation.org.uk/help-advice/abdominal-aortic-aneurysm/open-aaa-repair-operation/ (accessed on 10 May 2012).

Driving

If you have an aneurysm of 6.5 centimetres or bigger, you are legally unable to drive. If you have the aneurysm repaired by any type of surgery, you should be able to drive again, as long as you don’t have other health problems that limit your driving.[34]NHS Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm Screening Programme. What are the implications for driving?. Available at http://aaa.screening.nhs.uk/questions#fileid445 (accessed on 10 May 2012).

Travel

There are no set restrictions on travel when you have an aneurysm. This includes air travel, as aneurysms are no more likely to rupture in the air than on the ground. But some travel insurance companies are reluctant to insure people with an untreated aneurysm. Having an aneurysm repaired should resolve this problem.[35]NHS Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm Screening Programme. What are the implications for air travel and travel insurance? Available http://aaa.screening.nhs.uk/questions#fileid561 (accessed on 10 May 2012).

What are the effects of open repair on your quality of life?

Most people have a large decline in their usual activities and what they can do physically and socially within three weeks of open surgery. They also have a decrease in their energy level and may feel very tired.[36]Prinssen M, Buskens E, Blankensteijn JD; DREAM trial participants. Quality of life endovascular and open AAA repair. Results of a randomised trial. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2004; 27: 121-127.

Six weeks after surgery, most people still feel somewhat limited in these areas, particularly in their usual activities. However, their energy level has improved.[36]Prinssen M, Buskens E, Blankensteijn JD; DREAM trial participants. Quality of life endovascular and open AAA repair. Results of a randomised trial. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2004; 27: 121-127.

People who are asked about their mental health before and after surgery tend to say they feel better after surgery. They feel less distressed and have an increased sense of wellbeing. These improvements are still apparent one to two years after surgery.[36]Prinssen M, Buskens E, Blankensteijn JD; DREAM trial participants. Quality of life endovascular and open AAA repair. Results of a randomised trial. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2004; 27: 121-127.[39]EVAR Trial Participants. Endovascular aneurysm repair versus open repair in patients with abdominal aortic aneurysm (EVAR trial 1): randomised controlled trial. Lancet. 2005; 365: 2179-2186.

A year after open surgery, people tend to rate their general view of their health more highly than they did before surgery.[36]Prinssen M, Buskens E, Blankensteijn JD; DREAM trial participants. Quality of life endovascular and open AAA repair. Results of a randomised trial. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2004; 27: 121-127.

What are the risks of open repair?

An open aneurysm repair is a major operation and carries serious risks, including the chance of infection, heavy bleeding, and reactions to the general anaesthetic. Open surgery also can cause heart and breathing problems, and stroke. Between 2 in 100 and 6 in 100 people having open surgery have a heart attack or abnormal heart rhythms.[41]Qamar AS. Abdominal aortic aneurysm: a comprehensive review. Experimental & Clinical Cardiology. 2011; 16: 11-15.

Around 4 in 100 people die during open surgery or in the 30 days after surgery.[29]United Kingdom EVAR Trial Investigators, Greenhalgh RM, Brown LC, et al. Endovascular versus open repair of abdominal aortic aneurysm. New England Journal of Medicine. 2010; 362: 1863-1871.

Your chance of dying may be higher or lower based on your health and age. In one group of people aged 80 and older, nearly 9 in 100 died in hospital after an open repair.[30]Biancari F, Catania A, D'Andrea V, et al. Elective endovascular vs. open repair for abdominal aortic aneurysm in patients aged 80 years and older: systematic review and meta-analysis. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2011; 42: 571-576. People who are younger and in good health have a lower risk.

There are also risks after successful surgery. These include:[44]Winterborn RJ, Amin I, Lyratzopoulos G, et al. Preferences for endovascular (EVAR) or open surgical repair among patients with abdominal aortic aneurysms under surveillance. Journal of Vascular Surgery. 2009; 49: 576-581.[45]Qamar AS. Abdominal aortic aneurysm: A comprehensive review. Experimental & Clinical Cardiology. 2011; 16: 11-15.

  • An infection in your graft. This can happen soon after surgery or long after.
  • Kidney failure. Between 2 in 100 and 10 in 100 people have kidney failure after open surgery.[46]Greenberg RK, Chuter TA, Lawrence-Brown M, et al. Analysis of renal function after aneurysm repair with a device using suprarenal fixation (Zenith AAA Endovascular Graft) in contrast to open surgical repair. European Journal of Vascular and Endovascular Surgery. 2004; 39: 1219-1228.[47]Johnston KW. Multicenter prospective study of nonruptured abdominal aortic aneurysm. Part II.   • Problems with your bowels caused by a drop in blood flow. In rare cases, this can cause gangrene in a part of the bowel, which is serious.
  • Decreased circulation in the legs. This may cause pins and needles or pain in the legs when walking. Occasionally there can be severe leg pain even at rest.
  • Problems getting an erection or having sex. This often improves within three months.[48]Prinssen M, Buskens E, Tutein Nolthenius RP, et al. Sexual dysfunction after conventional and endovascular AAA repair: results of the DREAM Trial. Journal of Endovascular Therapy. 2004; 11: 613-620.

You may also have problems with your aneurysm after open repair, although most people do not. Within four years of surgery, 6 in 100 people need further treatment for their aneurysm.[39]EVAR Trial Participants. Endovascular aneurysm repair versus open repair in patients with abdominal aortic aneurysm (EVAR trial 1): randomised controlled trial. Lancet. 2005; 365: 2179-2186. [Chambers D, Epstein D, Walker S, et al. Endovascular stents for abdominal aortic aneurysms: a systematic review and economic model. Health Technology Assessement. 2009; 13: 1-189.] 

How will open repair affect my life?

You won’t need regular scans to check on your graft and aneurysm after open repair. However, you will probably have a follow-up appointment to check on your recovery. Your doctor can help you ease back into a more active lifestyle and advise you on when you are fit enough to return to work.

Your doctor may also recommend other treatments and lifestyle changes to improve your health. These may include losing weight, eating a healthier diet, stopping smoking, exercising, and taking medicines to lower your blood pressure or cholesterol. Some of these you may have started before you had surgery.

FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT ENDOVASCULAR ANEURYSM REPAIR

What is endovascular aneurysm repair?

This is a type of keyhole operation. It involves putting an artificial piece of artery, called a stent graft, inside your aorta to carry blood through the weakened section. The idea is to strengthen the part of the aorta with the aneurysm.

A surgeon makes small cuts in your groin at the top of your legs to reach large blood vessels. The graft is attached to a thin tube (a catheter). With the help of X-rays, the catheter guides the graft through the blood vessels and into the aneurysm. When the graft is in the right place, it is fastened with small hooks or pins.

‘Endovascular’ means the surgery is done through your blood vessels. You may sometimes hear endovascular aneurysm repair called EVAR for short.

You may have a general anaesthetic, so that you will be asleep during the operation. Or, you might have a regional or local anaesthetic plus sedation. This means you will be relaxed but awake during the surgery. You will not be able to feel the area being operated on.[50]Winterborn RJ, Amin I, Lyratzopoulos G,et al. Preferences for endovascular (EVAR) or open surgical repair among patients with abdominal aortic aneurysms under surveillance. Journal of Vascular Surgery. 2009; 49: 576-581.

After the operation, you will have regular scans to check on your aneurysm. This is to look for leaks around the graft and to make sure the graft is working properly and still in place. You will most likely have ultrasound scans, but in some hospitals you may have a CT scan instead (CT is short for computerised tomography).

You will probably have a scan six weeks, six months and a year after surgery, and then annually after that.[51]Department of Vascular Surgery. Queen Elizabeth Hospital Birmingham. Recovery from Endovascular Surgery (EVAR) for Aortic Aneurysm (AAA) Repair. Information for patients and carers. 2012. Available at http://www.uhb.nhs.uk/Downloads/pdf/PiRecoveryFromEndovascularAaaRepair.pdf (accessed 12 May 2012). If a problem is detected, you may need another operation. 

Who can choose endovascular repair?

Endovascular repair is not suitable for everyone.

Your blood vessels need to be a right size and shape for the operation. This is important so the graft can make a tight seal to prevent blood from leaking. If the aneurysm is too close to the blood vessels going to the kidneys, the operation could block the blood flow to these organs. Some people’s blood vessels are too narrow to allow the graft to be passed up to the aneurysm. Endovascular repair is an option for about half of people with an abdominal aortic aneurysm.[52]EVAR trial participants. Endovascular aneurysm repair versus open repair in patients with abdominal aortic aneurysm (EVAR trial 1): randomised controlled trial. Lancet 2005; 365: 2179-2186.

You must also be healthy enough to have the operation and benefit from it. If you have other serious health conditions, the operation may not help you live longer. So, it may not be worth the risks. Your age alone is unlikely to make a difference to whether you can choose endovascular repair.

What are the effects of endovascular repair on your risk of rupture?

Having endovascular repair lowers your risk of having your aneurysm rupture, compared with not having surgery. But it does not reduce the risk to zero.

In one group of people who had endovascular repair, around 3 in 100 people had a rupture during nearly five years of follow-up. Most of the ruptured aneurysms had signs of problems beforehand, such as blood leaking around the graft and aneurysm growth. This suggests that some of these ruptures might have been prevented with prompt treatment.[53]Wyss TR, Brown LC, Powell JT, et al. Rate and predictability of graft rupture after endovascular and open abdominal aortic aneurysm repair: data from the EVAR Trials. Annals of Surgery. 2010; 252: 805-12.

The risk of rupture for untreated aneurysms is higher. Within a year, up to 15 in 100 aneurysms measuring 5.5 to 6 centimetres will rupture. And the risk increases with aneurysm size. Up to 50 in 100 aneurysms 8 centimetres and larger will rupture within a year.[26]British Heart Foundation. Abdominal aortic aneurysms. Factfile No. 1. 2008. Available at http://www.bhf.org.uk/idoc.ashx?docid=f6657a6e-d1c7-485d-aaae-77e5868c6309&version=-1 (accessed 6 June 2012).

The risk of rupture is highest within 30 days of surgery. After that, it drops to a low, steady level.[53]Wyss TR, Brown LC, Powell JT, et al. Rate and predictability of graft rupture after endovascular and open abdominal aortic aneurysm repair: data from the EVAR Trials. Annals of Surgery. 2010; 252: 805-12.

What are the risks of endovascular repair?

There are risks in having endovascular repair. Some of these can happen during or soon after surgery. And some can happen later.

Problems during or soon after the operation

There is a chance of infection, bleeding and reactions to the anaesthetic with endovascular repair. It’s also possible to have a heart attack, stroke or breathing problems because of the surgery.

During the operation, there’s a chance that your aorta might be damaged, which could lead to the aneurysm bursting.

If there are problems during surgery, your surgeon may need to switch to an open repair. This is a bigger operation. Less than 1 in 100 endovascular repairs need to be switched to open surgery.[29]United Kingdom EVAR Trial Investigators, Greenhalgh RM, Brown LC, et al. Endovascular versus open repair of abdominal aortic aneurysm. New England Journal of Medicine. 2010; 362: 1863-1871.

It's quite common to find that you have problems with bowel movements in the period after your surgery. You may also find that you lose your appetite, which can lead to you losing weight. The wounds in the groin where the graft was put in may also take time to heal properly.

It’s possible to get problems with the blood flow to your bowel, kidneys, or legs after endovascular surgery. These problems aren’t common but they can be serious.

If you are a man, you might also have problems getting an erection or having sex soon after surgery. This often improves within six weeks.[48]Prinssen M, Buskens E, Tutein Nolthenius RP, et al. Sexual dysfunction after conventional and endovascular AAA repair: results of the DREAM Trial. Journal of Endovascular Therapy. 2004; 11: 613-620.

Nearly 2 in 100 people die during surgery or in the 30 days after having endovascular repair surgery.[29]United Kingdom EVAR Trial Investigators, Greenhalgh RM, Brown LC, et al. Endovascular versus open repair of abdominal aortic aneurysm. New England Journal of Medicine. 2010; 362: 1863-1871.

Problems related to the graft

These problems can happen soon after surgery or long after. They include:

  • Endoleaks. These are blood leaks between the graft and the wall of the aorta. Small blood vessels within the aneurysm wall can also leak. Endoleaks can cause the aneurysm to continue to grow and rupture.
  • The graft moving, becoming blocked, or breaking down. The graft may also become infected.

You will need long-term follow-up with regular scans to check on your aneurysm, look for leaks around the graft and make sure it is working properly and still in place. If a problem is found, you may need another operation. This could be an endovascular operation or open surgery.

Within four years, 20 in 100 people need additional treatment after endovascular repair.[39]EVAR Trial Participants. FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT SURVEILLANCE

What is surveillance?

If you have surveillance, you have regular ultrasound scans to check on your aneurysm. How frequently you have scans will depend on your individual circumstances and your aneurysm size.

At any time, you may decide to have surgery to repair your aneurysm. Or, you may continue to have surveillance long term.

The purpose of the ultrasound is to check whether your aneurysm is growing and how quickly. The larger your aneurysm becomes, the more likely it is to rupture. You also have a higher risk of rupture if your aneurysm is growing rapidly – for example, at a rate of more than 1 centimetre a year.[72]Nordon IM, Hinchliffe RJ, Loftus IM, et al. You might decide in advance that you’ll have surgery if your aneurysm reaches a certain size, or has a certain rate of growth. Or, you may want to weigh your risk of rupture against the risks of surgery each time you have a scan.

While having surveillance, your doctor may recommend lifestyle changes and treatments to help slow the growth of your aneurysm.

Lifestyle changes

  • Stopping smoking. Smoking is linked to faster aneurysm growth, but quitting smoking can make a difference.   • Taking regular exercise. Moderate exercise is safe for people with an abdominal aortic aneurysm. Exercise may play a role in slowing aneurysm growth, although we need more research to be sure. However, we do know exercise is good for your heart and blood flow in other ways and may lower your blood pressure. Taking regular exercise may also mean you’ll be more fit for surgery should you decide to have your aneurysm repaired. This may lower your risk of problems from the operation and shorten your recovery time.[75]Kothmann E, Batterham AM, Owen SJ, et al. Try to eat a heart-healthy, low-fat diet that includes lots of fruit, vegetables, and whole grains. If you are overweight, try to lose weight. And make sure you take any medicines that your doctor has prescribed, such as treatments for high blood pressure. 

Medicines

Several medicines have been studied as possible treatments to slow aneurysm growth, including some types of antibiotics and drugs that decrease inflammation in the body. Most of these are still considered experimental. You may be offered:

EFFECT OF TREATMENT ON YOUR LIFE

Open aneurysm repair

You won’t need regular scans to check on your graft and aneurysm after open repair. However, you will usually have a follow-up appointment to monitor your recovery. Your doctor can help you ease back into a more active lifestyle and advise you on when you are fit enough to return to work.

more information

Endovascular aneurysm repair

After you recover from surgery, you will need scans to check on your aneurysm and graft for the rest of your life. You will probably have a scan six weeks, six months, and a year after surgery, and then annually after that.[51]Department of Vascular Surgery. Queen Elizabeth Hospital Birmingham. Surveillance

You will need to schedule regular scans to check on your aneurysm. If you don’t live near the location of your appointments, you will have to factor in travel time.
