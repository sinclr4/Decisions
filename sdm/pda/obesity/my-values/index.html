Our Decision Support telephone service is here to assist and guide you through the Shared Decision Making process.

The Decision Support team is made of professional Health Coaches who are clinicians, commonly nurses, who have undertaken additional training to become a Health Coach.

The Health Coaches will assist you by:

  • Providing you with information
  • Helping you to understand your condition
  • Recognise what is important to you regarding the outcome of treatment
  • Identifying potential solutions
  • Encourage you in discussing options with your family
  • Transferring skills which will assist you in using the information and resources available to you
  • Support you in building confidence in discussing your choice with your doctor
  • You may find that this can be achieved in one telephone call with a Health Coach however; if further calls are required to support you in reaching your decision the Health Coach will schedule these with you.

Important Things To Note

  • A Health Coach does not know which treatment option is best for you.
  • The Health Coach does not have information about your medical history.
  • You will still need to talk over your decision and reasons for your decision with your doctor or nurse.

MORE INFORMATION ABOUT OBESITY

How does my body use food?

Food gives us the energy or ‘fuel’ we need to live. Different types of food contain different amounts of energy. The amount of energy in food is measured in calories (short for kilocalories, or kcal).

Your body needs energy all the time, even if you are not doing much. You need it just to keep warm and move around. The more active you are, the more energy you use.

Not everyone is the same when it comes to energy needs. The amount of energy you need depends on your metabolic rate - how quickly your body uses, or 'burns', the energy in the food you eat.

Your brain helps to keep your weight steady by balancing how much you eat with how much energy you burn. It sends out 'hungry' or 'full' signals that make you want to eat or stop eating.[1]Jequier E, Tappy L. Regulation of body weight in humans. Physiological Reviews. 1999; 79: 451-480.

What is obesity?

If someone is very overweight (healthcare professionals call this being obese) it means that their weight might cause them a health problem. The excess weight is made up of fat rather than muscle.

People can become very overweight when they take in many more calories from food and drink than their body uses for energy. The body stores those extra calories as fat, and people put on weight.

Being very overweight can cause major health problems, including:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Arthritis
  • Heart disease.

Getting back to a healthy weight can help people who are very overweight avoid these problems.

When healthcare professionals talk about a 'healthy' or 'ideal' weight, they mean a weight that shouldn’t cause serious health problems.[2]Sandowski SA. What is the ideal body weight? Family Practice. 2000; 17: 348-351.

Most doctors use the body mass index (BMI for short) to work out whether someone's weight is healthy.[3]National Institute for Clinical Excellence. Obesity: the prevention, identification, assessment and management of overweight and obesity in adults and children. December 2006. Available at http://www.nice.org.uk/Guidance/CG43 (accessed on 20 December 2012). [4]World Health Organization. Obesity: preventing and managing the global epidemic. Report of a WHO consultation. WHO Technical Series. World Health Organisation, Geneva. 2000; 894: 1-253. Your BMI is a single number that's worked out from your height and weight. You can calculate your own BMI using a formula. Your BMI is your weight in kilograms divided by your height in metres squared. If you find this complicated, you can find BMI calculators on the internet that will do the sums for you.

  • A BMI of less than 18.5 means you are underweight
  • A BMI of between 18.5 and 24.9 means your weight is normal
  • A BMI of between 25 and 29.9 means you are probably overweight
  • A BMI of 30 or more means you are probably obese (very overweight).

But someone's BMI score is not a perfect way to tell whether their weight might be causing them health problems.[5]Padwal RS, Pajewski NM, Allison DB, et al. Using the Edmonton obesity staging system to predict mortality in a population-representative cohort of people with overweight and obesity. Canadian Medical Association Journal. 2011; 183: 1059-1066. For example, some muscular athletes have BMI scores that suggest they are very overweight, because muscle is heavier than fat. That's misleading because being very overweight means that someone has too much body fat, not that they are muscular.

For most of us, though, working out our BMI is a quick and simple way of finding if our weight might cause health problems.

Why has this happened to me?

Many things can cause people to become very overweight.

Diet

People become very overweight because they eat more food than their body uses. The body stores this extra food as fat and, over time, people become overweight. There are many reasons why people eat more food than their body uses. Some foods have more calories than others. This means people can eat the same amount of food but get different amounts of 'fuel' from the different types of food.

To maintain our weight:

  • The average man needs to eat or drink about 2,500 calories a day
  • The average woman needs to eat or drink about 2,000 calories a day.

These figures are just averages, so it's not the same for everyone. People's bodies are different, and some people's bodies can use more calories from the same amount of food as someone else's. We don't know why this happens. It may have to do with our genes or hormones.

But these numbers are a useful rough guide for most people. There are many ways to find out how many calories are in the food we eat.

  • Food packaging. Many food labels will say how many calories the products contain.
  • Books. Pocket-sized calorie-counting books are available for a few pounds. They list how many calories are in different foods.
  • The internet. Calorie-counting websites can be a useful source of information on food.
  • Your doctor or practice nurse. Your local surgery or health centre may have information leaflets on finding out about calories.

Here are some examples of foods that have a lot of calories:

  • Fatty meat, and fatty foods in general
  • Sweets, chocolate, and other sugary foods
  • Cakes, pastries, pies, biscuits, crisps
  • Most cheeses
  • Fried foods
  • Processed meats (pate, sausages)
  • Fruit juice, soft drinks
  • Milk shakes, ice cream, cream
  • Most fast food
  • Alcoholic drinks

Here are some examples of foods that have fewer calories:

  • Lean meat
  • White fish
  • Most fruits
  • Most vegetables
  • Salads
  • Beans, lentils

Gaining weight might not just be about how much we eat. It's possible that some foods may be more likely than others to cause weight gain, regardless of how many calories they contain. There is growing evidence that sugar in particular may cause people to put on weight.[6]Te Morenga, L. Dietary sugars and body weight: systematic review and meta-analyses of randomised controlled trials and cohort studies. BMJ. 2013; 346: 7492. As well as sweets, cakes, and desserts, lots of processed foods, snacks, and soft drinks contain large amounts of sugar.

Changes in people's lives and lifestyles can affect their eating habits and how active they are. Some things that might increase someone's chances of becoming overweight include:

  • Childbirth (women are likely to be about a kilogram [2 pounds] heavier after each pregnancy, but many women lose this weight)
  • Illness
  • Trauma
  • Job changes.

Medicines

Some medicines can make people gain weight. These include some treatments for depression and anxiety (such as lithium, clozapine, and other antipsychotic drugs), corticosteroids, and tricyclic antidepressants.

You can talk to your healthcare professional about any medicines you are taking if you think they might be making you gain weight, or if you think you may be gaining weight because of a medical condition.

How do I find out I am very overweight?

If you think you may be heavier than is healthy for you, you can talk to your doctor or practice nurse. The doctor or nurse will weigh and measure you to work out your body mass index (BMI). People may also find out that they are very overweight if they are weighed during a visit to the doctor.

Apples and pears

Your doctor or nurse may also measure around your waist.[7]Noel PH, Pugh JA. Management of overweight and obese adults. BMJ. 2002; 325: 757-761.

You may have heard of people being either 'apple shaped' or 'pear shaped'. People who have a lot of body fat around their middle are considered 'apple shaped', and people who carry more of their body fat around their buttocks and thighs are considered 'pear shaped'.

So waist measurements are important because people who are apple shaped are more likely than people who are pear shaped to have heart problems caused by their weight.

How many people are very overweight?

Being very overweight (obese) is becoming more common in the UK. About 26 in 100 adults in the UK are obese.[8]NHS Information Centre. Statistics on obesity, physical activity and diet: England, 2012. February 2012. Available at http://www.ic.nhs.uk/ (accessed on 20 December 2012).

Twenty years ago this figure was 15 in 100.[9]National Obesity Observatory. Available at http://www.noo.org.uk/NOO_about_obesity/trends (accessed on 1 March 2013).

 

What will happen?

Being very overweight increases your chance of getting of some serious health problems. These include:[10]National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute. Clinical guidelines on the identification, evaluation and treatment of overweight and obesity in adults. Available at http://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/guidelines/obesity/ob_home.htm (accessed on 20 December 2012). [11]Rao G, Burke L, Spring B, et al. New and emerging weight management strategies for busy ambulatory settings: a scientific statement from the American Heart Association. Circulation. 2011; 124: 1182-11203.

  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Heart disease or stroke
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Osteoarthritis (stiff and painful joints)
  • Gallstones
  • Sleep apnoea (when you stop breathing for several seconds while asleep)
  • Some types of cancer
  • Menstrual problems.

Your chance of getting one or more of these conditions begins to rise when you become overweight and gradually increases as you get heavier. You are more likely to have some of these problems if you smoke or have relatives who have had heart problems.[7]Noel PH, Pugh JA. Management of overweight and obese adults. BMJ. 2002; 325: 757-761.

Being very overweight can shorten your life. People whose BMI is above 35 are about one third more likely than people whose BMI is in the normal range to die younger.[13]Flegal KM, Kit BK, Orpana H, et al. Association of all-cause mortality with overweight and obesity using standard body mass index categories. Journal of the American Medical Association. 2013. 309; 7-82. Adults who are very overweight live on average about seven years less than adults who are not overweight.[14]Peeters A, Barendregt JJ, Willekens F, et al. Obesity in adulthood and its consequences for life expectancy. Annals of Internal Medicine. 2003; 138: 24-32.

The more overweight people are and the more risk factors they have that affect their health (such as smoking or having relatives with heart disease), the more important it is for them to lose weight.

If all these facts and figures seem a little scary, or if you're worried about being able to lose much weight, the good news is that you may not have to lose a lot of weight for it to make a big difference to your health.

Losing as little as 5 percent of your weight can lower your chance of getting health problems linked to being very overweight.[15]Calle E, Thun MJ, Petrelli JM, et al. Body mass index and mortality in a prospective cohort of US adults. New England Journal of Medicine. 1999; 341: 1097-1105. That means someone who weighs 100 kilograms could reduce their risk of serious illness by losing 5 kilograms (about 11 pounds).

Being very overweight is a major cause of type 2 diabetes. Losing weight can stop many people getting type 2 diabetes in the first place.

People whose BMI is between 30 and 35 are advised to lose between 5% and 10% of their body weight. People whose BMI is greater than 35 are advised to lose more – between 15 percent and 20 percent of their body weight.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

If you are a woman who is very overweight and planning a family, it’s a good idea to lose weight before you become pregnant. Women who become pregnant when very overweight are more likely to have problems during pregnancy. These include pre-eclampsia, when your blood pressure can get very high and your baby may need to be delivered early.[17]Hauger MS, Gibbons L, Vik T, et al. Prepregnancy weight status and the risk of adverse pregnancy outcome. Acta Obstetricia et Gynecologica Scandinavica. 2008; 87: 953-959. Sensible diet and exercise during pregnancy can help keep you and your baby healthy.[18]Nascimento SL, Surita FG, Parpinelli MÂ, et al. The effect of an antenatal physical exercise programme on maternal/perinatal outcomes and quality of life in overweight and obese pregnant women a randomised clinical trial. BJOG: An International Journal of Obstetrics & Gynaecology. 2011; 118: 1455-1463. [19]Quinlivan JA, Julania S, Lam L. Antenatal dietary interventions in obese pregnant women to restrict gestational weight gain to institute of medicine recommendations: a meta-analysis. Obstetrics and Gynecology. 2011; 118: 1395-1401.

If you are very overweight and you lose weight, you may:

  • Lower your chance of having serious health problems
  • Help yourself to live longer
  • Feel more positive about yourself and your life.

There are many ways to lose weight and to stay healthy. These include:

  • Exercise and diet programmes
  • Medication
  • Psychological help (therapy)
  • Surgery (as a last resort).

Surgery is usually only recommended when other methods haven't worked well enough and someone's weight is affecting their health.

If you’re not sure where to get help, your doctor or practice nurse is a good place to start. The NHS supports several weight-loss programmes, and your healthcare professional can help you find one that suits you.

Remember, if you try treatments like psychological therapy or medications you still need to carry on exercising and eating sensibly. Diet and exercise should go hand in hand with any other treatments.

FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT LIFESTYLE CHANGES

What are lifestyle changes to lose weight?

Lifestyle changes are things people can do to lose weight without following a formal diet or exercise plan. You could think of it as a ‘do it yourself’ approach.

Lifestyle changes are the most important thing that very overweight people can do to make a difference to their health. Whatever other weight-loss treatments you choose, lifestyle changes go hand in hand with them to get the best results. The other treatments people can choose for weight loss won't work as well without making lifestyle changes.

Old habits can be hard to break. Forming new habits can take time. But you can choose the approach that works best for you. Some people find that starting gradually with small goals works for them, while others find it easier to 'dive in' and make big changes.[20]Casazza K, Fontaine KR, Astrup A, et al. Myths, Presumptions, and Facts about Obesity. New England Journal of Medicine. 2013; 368: 446-454. It's up to you. If one approach doesn't work for you, there are plenty of others.

Lifestyle changes that should help people lose weight include:

  • Eating less overall
  • Eating healthier foods
  • Eating lower-calorie foods
  • Exercising more
  • Weighing yourself regularly. Adults who do this are more likely to have control over their weight.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

The first aim of all these lifestyle changes is to help people lose weight. But how much weight loss makes a difference, and how much is realistic?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a number based on someone's weight and height that helps work out whether their weight is likely to cause them health problems. Broadly speaking, people whose BMI is between 30 and 35 are advised to lose between 5 percent and 10 percent of their body weight. People whose BMI is greater than 35 are advised to lose more - between 15 percent and 20 percent of their body weight.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

To lose this amount of weight most people need to do between four and five hours of exercise each week. The exercise should be of at least 'moderate intensity'. This means it should take more effort than ordinary activities. You should feel out of breath, and 'work up a sweat' - but without going overboard. People might do this kind of exercise by:

  • Gardening
  • Cycling
  • Doing aerobics or water aerobics
  • Jogging
  • Brisk walking
  • Playing team sports or tennis
  • Working out in a gym.

Even though you may be doing exercise on your own and not as part of a programme, it's a good idea to check with your healthcare professional before you start, to make sure you don't overdo it to begin with, and also to make sure you're doing enough. So you might start gently at first, but over time you will need to increase the amount you do.

People find it easier to exercise regularly if they're doing something they enjoy. For example, if going to a gym bores you, but you love the outdoors, then playing an outdoor sport, jogging, or walking briskly in the local park may suit you much better. Many people find they really enjoy exercising once they get the habit.

Exercise time can be broken up into daily amounts. And people don’t have to do all their daily exercise in one go. For example, you could do a few short sessions instead of one long one.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

People trying to lose weight through lifestyle changes should try to eat and drink about 600 calories a day less than they are using up through normal living and exercise. The average recommended daily intake of calories is 2,500 for men and 2,000 for women. People need to eat about 600 calories a day less than this until they've lost the weight they want to lose.

Learning about what foods make people put on weight can help.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012). Your family and friends can also help you.

Some people might find that setting themselves small realistic goals helps them to stay focused on losing weight. But other people do well by making big changes.[20]Casazza K, Fontaine KR, Astrup A, et al. Myths, Presumptions, and Facts about Obesity. New England Journal of Medicine. 2013; 368: 446-454. There isn't a right or wrong way to approach making lifestyle changes. It's about whatever works best for you.

Who can make lifestyle changes?

Anyone who is very overweight can make lifestyle changes. If you haven’t done much exercise in a while it may be a good idea to get advice from a healthcare professional about what sort of exercise is suitable for you to start with.

What are the main effects of lifestyle changes on being very overweight?

It’s hard to say for sure how well a do-it-yourself approach to weight loss works, because people who are treating themselves are not usually being studied. So we don’t have much evidence.

What we do know is that the long-term health benefits of regular exercise and sensible eating are well established. These benefits go way beyond helping you to lose weight.

Improving your diet and getting fit can help keep your heart healthy, and reduce your chances of getting many health problems.

Exercise and sensible eating can help you:

  • Keep to a healthy weight
  • Reduce your chances of getting serious health problems, such as type 2 diabetes, some cancers, and heart disease
  • Strengthen bones and build muscle
  • Get the right nutrition
  • Improve your mood
  • Sleep better
  • Reduce stress.
What are the main side effects of lifestyle changes?

As long as any changes you make to your diet and exercise habits are sensible, lifestyle changes shouldn’t have any side effects.

How might lifestyle changes affect my life?

As well as helping you lose weight, lifestyle changes can have important health benefits if you are very overweight.

  • Eating a healthy diet and being more active can reduce your chances of getting type 2 diabetes, or having heart problems.
  • You may feel fitter and able to do more.
  • You may feel more confident.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

But making lifestyle changes means getting used to new ways of doing things.

  • It means finding time to exercise. Many people find they enjoy exercising once they have got started.
  • You may have to plan your meals ahead to make sure you have the right things to eat.
  • Eating well may mean changing the way you shop and where you eat out. Your family and friends can help with this. You may find that some of them would like to get involved in losing weight and keeping fit and healthy, too.

FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT FORMAL WEIGHT-LOSS PROGRAMME

What are weight-loss programmes to lose weight?

A formal weight-loss programme is a plan that people follow to help them lose weight. It could be a diet or exercise plan, or a mixture of both. Programmes that include both exercise and diet plans are more effective than programmes that just focus on one or the other.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012). And programmes that also include some psychological support work even better. These programmes help people change their behaviour and how they think about food.

Your doctor can help you find a plan that suits you. They are likely to suggest a diet plan that involves eating fewer high-calorie, fatty, and sugary foods, and a sensible exercise plan. If you choose to work out a weight-loss programme with your healthcare professional, you can have follow-up appointments to see how you are doing. Your doctor may also want to test things that are affected by your weight, such as cholesterol and blood sugar levels.

You should be able to get involved with weight-loss programmes in your local area. You could join a programme that is run or supported by the NHS. Or you could join a commercial weight-loss programme, but you will have to pay for these.

Some weight-loss programmes are listed below. These are not the only ones available, but they are examples of the kind of programmes available and should give you an idea of the kinds of help you can get. These programmes don't work for everyone, and some of them will suit some people better than others. You may have heard of some of them already, and have an idea of what kind of programme might work for you.

  • Shape Up. Programmes like this one, which is run by the NHS, focus on exercise, healthy eating, and behavioural support to help change the way people think about food.
  • Counterweight. Programmes like this one aim to help people lose weight and keep it off, through diet, exercise, and changing the way people think about food. These programmes sometimes involve replacing foods with 'formula' foods in the early stages. Formula foods are meal replacements that can be drinks, soups, or snack bars that contain nutrients but very few calories. If you want to try one of these very low-calorie formula diets, it's a good idea to talk to your doctor first.

The most successful programmes are those that contain all three of the elements necessary to lose weight and keep it off:

  • Diet. This usually means eating less overall, eating less-fattening foods, and eating healthier foods. In the early stages of a programme, it may mean replacing some or all meals with formula foods.
  • Exercise. Exercise on its own may not help people lose a lot of weight. But it is crucial for good health in people who are very overweight, and for keeping weight off once you've lost it.
  • Psychological and behavioural support. Changing how we think about food, and understanding our eating habits, can help many people change their behaviour.

Which diet?

Below is a list of some of the most common diet plans.

  • Low fat
  • Low calorie
  • Very low calorie (these may include 'formula' foods, or meal replacements - it's a good idea to talk to your doctor if you want to try one of these diets)
  • Low carbohydrate

All of these diets have been shown to help people lose weight.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012). But everyone is different, and some diet plans simply won't 'feel' right to some people. Different methods work for different people, perhaps depending on their lifestyle.

How much exercise?

A formal exercise programme should include between four and five hours of exercise each week. If this sounds like a lot, remember that, to begin with, exercise doesn’t have to mean running, playing sports, or going to a gym. You can start slowly - especially if you haven't done much exercise for a while - and then do more over time.

A sensible exercise programme, such as one you might get from your healthcare professional, will take account of things like your age, your weight, and how fit you are.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

People find it easier to exercise regularly if they're doing something they enjoy. For example, if going to a gym bores you, but you love the outdoors, then playing an outdoor sport, or jogging or brisk walking in the local park may suit you much better. Many people find they really enjoy exercising once they get the habit.

Who can try a weight-loss programme?

Most people can try a weight-loss programme. There are some things to think about before you start.

  • If you haven’t exercised for a while, you may want to take it easy at first before you build up to doing more-intense exercise. Your healthcare professional can suggest sensible ways to exercise.
  • Some diets are not suitable for everyone. If you have diabetes, or if you are thinking of trying a very low-calorie diet, you should talk to your healthcare professional first.

National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012). What are the main side effects of weight-loss programmes?

A sensible weight-loss programme should not cause side effects.

 

How might weight-loss programmes affect my life?

As well as helping you lose weight, formal weight-loss programmes can have important health benefits if you are very overweight.

  • Eating a healthy diet and being more active can reduce your chances of getting type 2 diabetes, or having heart problems.
  • Being active may help you feel more confident and more able to socialise.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).
  • If you have problems with mobility (getting around and doing things), losing weight could make a big difference.
  • Exercising may make you feel fitter and able to do more. The health benefits of exercise go beyond helping you lose weight.

But following a weight-loss programme does mean making some changes.

  • It means finding time to exercise. Many people find they enjoy exercise once they get started.
  • You may find that the foods on your diet plan cost more than those you usually eat. Going out with friends and family may be harder if you can’t eat and drink what you used to.
  • You may have to plan your meals ahead to make sure you have the right things to eat. Eating well may mean changing the way you shop and where you eat out. Your family and friends can help with this. You may find that some of them would like to get involved in losing weight and keeping fit and healthy, too.

FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT PSYCHOLOGICAL TREATMENTS

What are psychological treatments to lose weight?

When healthcare professionals talk about psychological treatments they usually mean so-called 'talking therapies'. These treatments involve a person talking with a trained therapist to get help with a problem.

The treatments used most often are:

  • Behavioural therapy
  • Cognitive behavioural therapy.

For people who are very overweight, the therapist aims to help give people a new set of skills to help them change their diet and become more active. 

  • Therapy often includes talking about your feelings, including how you feel about your body shape, your weight, and eating.
  • If you binge on food because you're unhappy or stressed, your therapist can help you deal with that.
  • The changes shouldn't end when therapy stops. With time and practice, people can learn to help themselves whenever they feel their old eating habits taking over again.

People often have behavioural therapy or cognitive behavioural therapy as part of a group of about 10 people. They meet for one or two hours each week, for between three and five months. They often do homework between sessions.

Behavioural therapy can involve some or all of the following:

  • Self-monitoring means keeping track of your habits so you become more aware of what you eat.[45]Glenny AM, O'Meara S, Melville A, et al. The treatment and prevention of obesity: a systematic review of the literature. International Journal of Obesity. 1997; 21: 715-737. It often means keeping a food diary. Each day you write down what you eat, when and where it was, and how you felt at the time. You and your therapist can then talk about the times when you have eaten too much or reached for high-calorie snacks. You can talk about ways of avoiding this in the future.
  • Stimulus control involves learning the ‘cues’ or triggers in your social life or home life that make you want to eat too much. You then learn to avoid or change these cues. For example, you may overeat whenever you see a certain friend. Meeting the friend in a place where there isn't any food removes the cue.
  • Reinforcement means rewarding yourself for making positive changes. For example, you might decide not to eat just before bed time for the next 10 days. If you succeed, you might buy yourself a small present.
  • Social support means trying to lose weight with a friend or family member. It can also mean making sure that other people around you are helping your efforts.
  • Cognitive behavioural therapy means you look at the way your thoughts affect how you feel and what you do. You learn to challenge unhelpful thoughts and think in a more positive way.

Other sources of psychological treatment

The therapies mentioned above are not the only psychological treatments available for people who are very overweight. Many people get psychological support in other ways:

  • Many general weight-loss programmes, including some that you can access through your doctor or practice nurse, include specific psychological and behavioural support.
  • Commercial weight-loss programmes often include psychological and behavioural support. For example, programmes that uses formula foods to help people lose weight very quickly may also include counselling and behavioural help.
  • Talking therapies don't have to come through the NHS. You can see a therapist privately if you prefer (but you will have to pay for this treatment).
  • Dietitians, nurses, and other health professionals can be a source of psychological support and counselling.
Who can try psychological treatments?

Most people can access some kind of psychological support to help them lose weight. You can get this help in several ways:

  • From your doctor or practice nurse. They might be able to refer you for talking treatments such as cognitive behavioural therapy.
  • Through weight-loss programmes you can access through the NHS that contain an element of psychological and behavioural help.
  • Through commercially available weight-loss programmes that contain an element of psychological and behavioural help
  • Through 'going private' for talking therapies
  • Through a nurse, dietitian, or other health professional. Your doctor may be able to direct you to the person who can help most.
What are the main effects of psychological treatments on being very overweight?

Psychological treatments, such as behavioural therapy and cognitive behavioural therapy, help people lose about the same amount of weight as a diet plan after one year (about 5 percent, or one twentieth, of their body weight).[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012). [47]Shaw KA, O’Rourke P, Del Mar C, et al. Psychological interventions for overweight or obesity (Cochrane review). In: The Cochrane Library. Wiley, Chichester, UK.

People who are very overweight lose more weight if they combine psychological treatment with a diet plan than if they use a diet plan alone. Combining psychological treatment with both diet and exercise plans leads to the most weight loss.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

Involving a partner or family member in psychological treatment may help people lose more weight than doing it on their own.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

What are the main side effects of psychological treatments?

Psychological treatments to help you lose weight are unlikely to have side effects.

How might psychological treatments affect my life?

If psychological treatment helps you to change the way you think about food and about your body:

  • You may feel more in control of your eating habits
  • Your mood may improve and you may feel more positive about losing weight
  • You may find it easier to change your behaviour in the long term.

But there are a few things to remember.

  • If you have psychological treatments to help you lose weight, you will have to find time to attend appointments. You may have an appointment every week, or every two weeks, for several months.
  • People get the best results from psychological treatment when they combine it with other measures to lose weight, such as a diet and exercise programme. So you will still need to make lifestyle changes to get the most from psychological treatments.

FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT MEDICATION

What is medication to lose weight?

This means taking a drug called orlistat that can help people lose weight. It stops your body absorbing some of the fat from the food you eat.

This doesn't mean that people who take orlistat can eat whatever they like and still lose weight. The medicine only works alongside a low-calorie, low-fat diet.

You can get orlistat capsules on prescription from your doctor under the brand name Xenical. You can also buy orlistat from a pharmacist, without a prescription, under the brand name Alli. The pills you can buy from the pharmacist contain half the dose of the prescription version.

Pharmacists will be able to help with any questions about over-the-counter orlistat.

Who can have medication?

If you want to get orlistat from your doctor, they might advise you to try sensible diet and exercise first.

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a number based on someone's weight and height that helps work out whether their weight is likely to cause them health problems. Doctors may recommend orlistat if someone's BMI is at least 28 and they have medical problems caused by their weight, or their BMI is at least 30.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

You can buy low-dose orlistat from a pharmacist if:

  • You are 18 or over
  • Your BMI is 28 or more
  • You are willing to start a low-calorie, lower-fat diet.

Your pharmacist will be able to help with any questions you have about over-the-counter orlistat, and to give you advice about how to use it. Pharmacists may be able to help with many questions about weight loss.

Like any treatment for weight loss, orlistat will only work well if used alongside lifestyle changes, such as exercising and eating sensibly.

It's especially important that people who take orlistat also eat a low-fat diet. This is because if you take orlistat after a meal that contains a lot of fat you are likely to have diarrhoea.

People who take orlistat need to avoid foods that contain a lot of fat. No meal should contain more than 15 grams of fat. It's not always easy to weigh and measure all the fat in your food, so it's best to avoid very fatty foods altogether.[53]Royal Pharmaceutical Society of Great Britain. Practice Guidance: OTC Orlistat. 2009. Available at http://rps.koha-ptfs.co.uk/cgi-bin/koha/opac-detail.pl?biblionumber=19867&shelfbrowse_itemnumber=20109 (accessed on 1 March 2013).

Fatty foods include things like:

  • Fried or deep-fried food
  • Butter, full-fat milk, margarine, cheese, cream, and ice cream
  • Fatty meat, such as burgers, sausages, ham, bacon, and fatty chops
  • Cakes and pastries.

It's best to stick to lean meat, fresh vegetables, salads, and fruit. Many processed foods, such as ready meals, will tell you on the packaging how much fat they contain.

People usually take orlistat for 12 weeks. Doctors will recommend that people keep taking it only if they lose at least 5 percent (one twentieth) of their body weight in the first 12 weeks. If this doesn’t happen, the medicine isn’t working well enough and there is no point in taking more.

Orlistat can help people lose weight. People who are very overweight who take prescription orlistat and follow a diet plan lose more weight than people who just follow a diet plan.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

If you take prescription orlistat, about one third of the fat you eat passes straight out of your body in your stools when you go to the toilet. The low-dose orlistat you can buy from a pharmacy can also help you lose weight, but we can't say for sure how much it helps. There hasn’t been as much research about over-the-counter orlistat as there has about the prescription medicine.[55]Smith SRS, Berk V, et al. The main side effect of orlistat is diarrhoea. Most people who take orlistat have diarrhoea at some point.[56]Li Z, Maglione M, Tu W, et al. Meta-analysis: pharmacologic treatment of obesity. Annals of Internal Medicine. 2005; 142: 532-546.

You are more likely to have diarrhoea with orlistat if you eat a high-fat meal.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

How might medication affect my life?

If orlistat helps you lose weight, there may be several benefits.

  • You may feel more positive and able to do more.
  • You may be less likely to have health problems that can go with being very overweight.
  • If you have problems with mobility (getting around and doing things) then losing weight could make a big difference.

Orlistat can help you lose weight, but there are some things you need to remember.

  • If you take orlistat you will also need to follow a low-calorie, low-fat diet, and continue to exercise.
  • You'll need to remember to take your medicine when you are supposed to.
  • Eating fatty foods while taking orlistat is likely to cause nausea or diarrhoea.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).
  • You will have to plan your meals and be careful about what you eat when you go out.
  • If you buy orlistat from a pharmacist without a prescription it will usually cost between £12 and £15 for a week’s supply.

FURTHER INFORMATION ABOUT BARIATRIC SURGERY

What is bariatric surgery to lose weight?

Some people who are very overweight may be able to have surgery. This treatment will not be suitable or available for most people who are very overweight. Most people who have surgery have tried other treatments first.

Bariatric surgery usually means having an operation to change the way the stomach deals with food. The exception is having an intra-gastric balloon, which does not need an operation.

Many types of bariatric surgery have been used over the years. The ones that work best and are most used are:

  • Gastric banding. The surgeon puts a thin band around the stomach, which divides it into two parts. The top part of the stomach becomes a small pouch that can hold only a few ounces of food. This means you will be able to eat much less. Banding is usually done with laparoscopic (‘keyhole’) surgery, where the surgeon makes a series of small incisions (cuts) rather than one big one. Gastric bands are usually adjustable. This means the surgeon can make the band tighter or looser by adding or removing salt water. When the band is tightened, food passes out of the pouch very slowly and you stay full for longer. When the band is loosened, food passes more quickly through the digestive system. The band is loose to start with and then tightened as people become used to eating less.
  • Gastric bypass. This is an operation to make the part of the stomach that deals with food smaller and the bowels (intestines) shorter. You then absorb fewer calories from the food you eat.
  • Sleeve gastrectomy. The surgeon uses staples to divide the stomach into two. One side becomes a narrow tube to carry the food. The other side is no longer used. When you eat, the food goes into the narrow tube and leaves through your intestine (the tube that takes food away from your stomach) as normal. The intestine can now take only a few ounces of food. This means you should feel full sooner than usual. You will need to stop eating once you feel full.
  • Intra-gastric balloon. Having an intra-gastric balloon fitted is not really surgery, as it can usually be done through the mouth without the need for an incision (cut). The balloon is made of soft silicone and filled with air or sterile salt water. When it is full it takes up a lot of space in the stomach, so you don’t need to eat much before you feel full. The balloon is usually taken out after six months.
Who can have bariatric surgery?

Bariatric surgery is not suitable or available for most people who are very overweight.

Even if someone is eligible for surgery, healthcare professionals will suggest that they try to lose weight using diet and exercise first, and that they think seriously about whether they are really willing to have surgery. This is because this kind of surgery can cause serious complications, and because people need to make major life changes afterwards.

Despite the risks, demand for bariatric surgery is high. This means that there are waiting lists. Waiting times will vary from area to area, but the wait is likely to be at least several months.

A person's Body Mass Index (BMI) is a number based on their weight and height that helps work out whether their weight is likely to cause them health problems. Healthcare professionals will usually only suggest surgery if:[3]National Institute for Clinical Excellence. Obesity: the prevention, identification, assessment and management of overweight and obesity in adults and children. December 2006. Available at http://www.nice.org.uk/Guidance/CG43 (accessed on 20 December 2012).

  • Your BMI is at least 40, or your BMI is between 35 and 40 and you have other health problems such as diabetes or high blood pressure
  • You've tried hard to lose weight in other ways (dieting and exercising, or taking orlistat, for example) for at least six months, but none of these treatments has worked
  • You've been treated and supported by experts in a hospital obesity clinic but still haven't lost weight and kept it off
  • You are prepared to commit to long-term follow-up appointments, for years after the operation

If your BMI is greater than 50, your doctor might recommend surgery before trying other weight-loss methods.

People also need to be fit enough to have a general anaesthetic and an operation. Healthcare professionals will want to be sure that people are committed to changing the way they eat afterwards.

The type of surgery or procedure people are offered may depend on several things. For example, someone may be offered an intra-gastric balloon if they are too overweight to have surgery. They may be offered one type of surgery because there is a surgeon in their area who has a lot of experience of doing that kind of operation.

What are the main effects of bariatric surgery on being very overweight?

Bariatric surgery doesn't work for everyone who has it, but it can help people lose a lot of weight, and the effects can last a long time. Ten years after having bariatric surgery, most people will have lost and kept off, between half and three-quarters of their excess weight (the amount of weight above the level that would be unlikely to cause them health problems).[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

How quickly people lose weight after surgery can depend on the type of surgery they have. People who have a gastric band fitted usually lose between one and two pounds a week for between a year and 18 months.

People who have a gastric bypass may lose weight more quickly than this to begin with, but over time the weight loss will be about the same as with a gastric band.[64]NHS Choices. Is weight loss surgery right for me? Available at http://www.nhs.uk/Livewell/loseweight/Pages/weight-loss-surgery.aspx (accessed on 1 March 2013).

Intra-gastric balloons are usually removed after six months. We don’t know if they help people lose weight in the longer term. These balloons are shorter-term measures that are sometimes used to help people lose weight before they have surgery, if they are extremely overweight.

People who follow an exercise programme after having surgery lose more weight than those who don’t exercise.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

People are more likely to regain weight after surgery if their BMI is greater than 50 before they have surgery.[66]Attiah MA, Halpern CH, Balmuri U, et al. Durability of Roux-en-Y bypass surgery. Annals of Surgery. 2012; 256: 251-254.

People who are very overweight who have bariatric surgery reduce their chance of dying over the next 10 years by about one third.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. What are the main side effects of bariatric surgery?

All types of surgery have risks. It is important to understand what might happen.

About 13 in 100 people have problems soon after bariatric surgery. These can include infection, bleeding, blood clots in the legs (deep-vein thrombosis), blood clots in the lungs (pulmonary embolism), and problems with the wound.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

Between 2 in 100 and 3 in 100 people need a second operation because of complications with the first one.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

Between 2 in 1,000 and 3 in 1,000 people die in the three months after having bariatric surgery, compared with 1 in 1,000 people of a similar weight who don’t have surgery. One year after surgery there is no difference in numbers of deaths between those who have surgery and those who don't.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

Some people who lose a lot of weight after bariatric surgery find that they have a lot of loose skin some months after the operation, especially around the breasts, tummy, hips, arms, and legs. This is more likely to happen if you were very overweight for many years before your operation.

People usually notice these folds of skin between 12 and 18 months after surgery, as they lose more weight. Many people don’t like how their skin looks when this happens. These folds of skin can be hard to keep clean. You may be more likely to get rashes and infections.

You can have cosmetic surgery to remove this excess skin. This treatment is not available on the NHS and you would have to pay for it yourself. Skin-removal treatment costs between £1,500 and £6,000, depending on the amount to be removed.[72]National Health Service. NHS Choices. Available at http://www.nhs.co.uk (accessed on 20 December 2012).

Bariatric surgery can have side effects that happen some time after the operation. These include:[72]National Health Service. NHS Choices. Available at http://www.nhs.co.uk (accessed on 20 December 2012).

  • Gallstones. About 8 in 100 people get gallstones after bariatric surgery, usually about 10 months afterwards. In most people, these don’t cause any symptoms. But in some people they can irritate the gallbladder, causing pain, nausea, and jaundice (yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes). If this happens to you, you will need an operation to remove your gallbladder.
  • Stomal stenosis. This is when a piece of food blocks the hole (stoma) that connects the stomach to the intestine. Over time, this happens in about 20 in 100 people who have a gastric bypass operation. The main symptom of stomal stenosis is vomiting.
  • Gastric band slippage. This can happen to about 2 in 100 people who have gastric band surgery. The band slips out of place, making the stomach pouch bigger than it should be. This can cause nausea, vomiting, and heartburn. If this happens to you, you will need an operation to put it right.
  • Nutritional deficiencies. After surgery you may not absorb all the nutrients you need from the small amount of food that your stomach can now hold. You will probably have to take vitamin supplements, possibly for the rest of your life.
  • Vomiting. This may happen until you learn how much food your smaller stomach can manage. You may have to chew your food for longer before swallowing.
  • Hernias. This may happen after surgery if the organs in your abdomen, such as the intestines, move out of their normal positions when you lose weight quickly. It can be corrected by surgery.
  • Ulcers. This is more likely to happen after a gastric bypass than with other techniques. If you have pain that carries on for some time after the operation, speak to your doctor.
How might bariatric surgery affect my life?

People who have bariatric surgery usually have a lot of excess weight.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).

If having bariatric surgery helps you lose a lot of weight:

  • You are likely to be more mobile
  • You may avoid some health problems that can go with being very overweight
  • You will have lower blood sugar and cholesterol levels than people of the same weight who don’t have surgery.[16]Scottish Intercollegiate Guidelines Network. National Clinical Guideline 115: Management of obesity. 2010. Available at http://www.sign.ac.uk/guidelines/fulltext/115/index.html (accessed on 20 December 2012).
  • You may feel fitter and more able to exercise.

But there are many things to think about before deciding to have surgery:

  • Having this kind of surgery is not like having an operation that cures an illness once and for all. People who have this surgery cannot eat in the same way afterwards, and they need to exercise regularly. So surgery is not a one-time cure for being very overweight. It's the start of a treatment and lifestyle programme for life.
  • If you choose to have bariatric surgery and your doctor agrees it’s best for you, you will probably have to follow a diet plan for several months before the operation.
  • Most types of bariatric surgery can be done using ‘keyhole’ surgery (with small cuts) rather than open surgery (with a larger cut). But people who are extremely overweight may not be able to have keyhole surgery. Open surgery is a bigger operation. People who have open surgery may need to spend longer in hospital and will take longer to heal.

Recovery times are slightly different for the different types of surgery.

Gastric band surgery

  • When people wake after gastric band surgery, they may feel some pain around the wound. This is normal and may last a few days. People may be given a blood-thinning medicine to stop them getting blood clots in their legs (deep vein thrombosis) or lungs (pulmonary embolism). These problems happen to less than 1 in 100 people.[76]Brasileiro AL, Miranda F Jr, Ettinger JE, et al. Incidence of lower limbs deep vein thrombosis after open and laparoscopic gastric bypass. Obesity Surgery. 2008; 18: 52-57.
  • For the first 24 hours people will only be allowed to drink small amounts of liquids. This is because the stomach will be swollen and sore from the surgery. Gradually people can start eating small amounts of food.
  • Most people can leave hospital within two days and will feel back to normal in between four and six weeks.
  • By this time, the stomach should have healed enough for the band to be adjusted. This is done by numbing the abdomen with a local anaesthetic, using an x-ray to find the band, then pumping salt water into the band through an opening called an access port. This all takes about 15 minutes.
  • It can take a bit of trial and error to adjust a gastric band to the right level. If the upper pouch is too large, it may not result in much weight loss. If the upper pouch is too small, it might cause vomiting after eating. The band may need to be adjusted several times before it works properly.

Gastric bypass surgery

  • When people wake after gastric bypass surgery, they will probably have a drip in their arm, to provide their body with liquids, and a tube in the bladder, known as a urinary catheter, to drain urine.
  • These tubes will be removed when someone is well enough to get out of bed. They may be given blood-thinning medicine to help prevent blood clots. They can only drink small amounts of liquids at first. They will gradually start to eat small amounts of solid food.
  • Most people are well enough to leave hospital between three and six days after surgery, and they recover completely in between three and five weeks.

With sleeve gastrectomy most people stay in hospital for three or four days, and can return to normal activities again between four and six weeks later.

Fitting a gastric balloon can usually be done without a surgeon making an incision. People should be able to leave hospital on the same day and be back to normal almost straight away.

After banding, bypass, or gastrectomy people need to have long-term regular check-ups, including blood tests. For the first few months after surgery people may have symptoms, such as tiredness and aching, as the body gets used to losing a lot of weight quickly.

People who have surgery will need to follow a strict eating plan for eight weeks after their operation. After that, they need to follow a long-term sensible diet plan. They may have to take some supplements (vitamins and minerals) for the rest of their lives.

If, some months after surgery, someone has a lot of loose skin after losing a lot of weight, they may decide they want cosmetic surgery to remove it. This can be expensive because the treatment is not available on the NHS.
